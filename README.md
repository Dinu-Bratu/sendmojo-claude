# SendMojo

*A community for sending lightweight mojo when someone needs it most.*

## Overview

SendMojo is built around a simple idea.

When life occasionally delivers moments that leave us struggling, grieving, regretting a missed opportunity, worrying, or simply hoping for a little encouragement, other people can respond with support by sending mojo.

A mojo request may be written for yourself, on behalf of a friend or family member, or for someone whose hardship you simply happened to witness. Sometimes the person receiving the mojo may never know the request was made—and that's perfectly okay.

Examples:

> **TITLE:** My feline friend is gone
**TEXT:** My 12-year-old cat Gibson died this weekend. I'm so bummed. Send mojo.

> **TITLE:** The &dollar;950 Mistake
**MESSAGE:** A &dollar;2,500 Takamine was marked down to &dollar;950. I hesitated. Someone else didn't. Send mojo. 

> **TITLE:** I'm unemployed...again!
**MESSAGE:** Just got laid off, along with 30 other co-workers. Now I'm unemployed. Send mojo. 

> **TITLE:** Hello, offseason
**MESSAGE:** My team just got killed in the playoffs today. Send mojo 

> **TITLE:** A sad scene in Seattle
**MESSAGE:** I just witnessed a lonely homeless man having a mental health crisis. Please send mojo.

A mojo request is a brief invitation for others to pause, acknowledge that moment, and send a little goodwill through the cosmos.

The requests are intentionally concise. The responses are intentionally concise. The goal is not to tell an entire life story, but to create small moments of empathy, acknowledgment, and human connection that can be shared in just a few words.

Respondents acknowledge the request and may share a brief personal connection of their own:

> I lost my dog Buster two years ago. It was devastating. Mojo sent.

> I've been there too, man. Mojo sent. We all have one perfect guitar that got away.

> Mojo sent. Being laid off is brutal. Made me feel like a tiny cog in a huge machine.

> My heart goes out to every mentally ill homeless person I see wandering the streets. Mojo sent.

Some responses may be only the two words **Mojo sent.** Others may include a brief message of empathy, encouragement, or a similar personal experience. The acknowledgment itself is part of the ritual—the responder has not only thought about the requester, but has also left behind a visible affirmation that the mojo was sent.

Sometimes, knowing that another person paused for a moment and thought about someone else is enough.

## Inspiration

The idea originates from the old Harmony Central music community, where users would frequently conclude difficult or emotional posts with a simple request:

> Send Mojo.

Other members would respond:

> Mojo sent.

Over time, this became a community ritual. The phrase simply became a concise way of expressing support and solidarity.

SendMojo seeks to preserve and modernize that tradition.

As a deliberate nod to Harmony Central, the platform may include category tags related to some of the old site's most popular historical discussion areas, including guitars, guitar pedals, and amplifiers.

## Design Principles

SendMojo is intentionally different from traditional social media platforms.

Its core principles include:

* Requests matter more than requesters.
* Responses matter more than responders.
* Public profiles are anonymized and ephemeral.
* No follower model.
* No influencer model.
* No reputation scores.
* No popularity rankings.
* No trending algorithms.
* No engagement farming.

The goal is to encourage empathy, not attention-seeking.

## Project Philosophy

Requests and responses have identities.

People do not.

SendMojo is designed to help people *anonymously* connect around shared experiences rather than build public reputations.

In a world increasingly optimized for attention, SendMojo aims to create a small place optimized for acknowledgment.

Anonymous: **Send mojo.**
Anonymous: **Mojo sent.**
